Google LLC is an American multinational technology company, a subsidiary of Alphabet Inc., founded by Larry Page and Sergey Brin in 1998. Initially developed as a research project called "BackRub" at Stanford University, the company became famous for its influential search engine, which organized the world's information. Today, Google offers a wide range of products and services, including Google Search, email (Gmail), cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and consumer electronics, making it one of the most powerful and valuable companies globally. 
Founding and History
Founders: Larry Page and Sergey Brin, then Ph.D. students at Stanford University, launched Google on September 4, 1998. 
Origins: The search engine's precursor was a research project called "BackRub". 
Name: The name "Google" is a play on the mathematical term "googol," representing a 1 followed by 100 zeros. 
Alphabet Inc.: In 2015, Google became the main subsidiary of the newly formed parent holding company, Alphabet Inc. 
Core Business and Products
Google Search: The company's flagship product, it is the most widely used search engine worldwide. 
Digital Services: Google offers numerous other services, including Google Maps, Gmail, Google Chrome, and a suite of cloud computing services. 
Technology and AI: The company is heavily invested in developing artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and other cutting-edge technologies. 
Hardware: Google also produces hardware, such as consumer electronics, including its own smartphones and smart home devices. 
Impact and Mission
Organizing Information: Google's core mission has been to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful. 
Internet Influence: Google has been a fundamental force in the development of the internet, and the term "googling" has become synonymous with searching online. 
Global Reach: The company is one of the world's most valuable and influential technology corporations, impacting billions of users daily.
